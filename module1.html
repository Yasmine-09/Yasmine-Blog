Self-Awareness Activity: Who was I back then?

Module 1

Challenges that shaped you

Forced Maturity
I couldn’t foresee a life where I’d do everything alone—no helper, no mom, no one to call my own. I was the epitome of sheltered and dependent, raised where everything was given, easy and convenient. I couldn’t cook, do laundry, or travel on my own, even crossing streets or going out felt unknown. But studying miles away pulled me out of that zone—homesick nights made me feel how far I had grown. When I’m sick, it’s just me, no comfort, no guide; adjustment hits hardest when you’ve no one by your side.

Friendships that grounded you

A Humbling Kinship
In life, you meet or lose people. This time, I experienced both. I’ve always held high standards in friendship, because to be my best friend means a bond like blood, rare to find. Even when I knew you when I was 8, it’s a matter of trust to be one. I met people I thought I would finally consider my bestfriend, yet life was inevitably cruel that it throw us obstacles that I never thought would ruin the friendship we tried to build. For context, I was never treated as someone’s favorite friend or the one-call away. Therefore, I built walls no one can cross, but when I met Y and K, I thought, I’ll finally let my guards down but I was unfortunately mistaken. I was cut off. Maybe because I’m the disney princess they don’t want— someone they must carry, teach, bare to understand, and constantly adjust for. Still, I’ve learned to be intentional with who I let in, because to me, real friendship is rare—and not everyone is meant to stay that long.

Failures that humbled you

Missed Horizons
It’s not really a failure, but a quiet regret—the realization that I once thought life was only about academics, about grades, and about making my parents proud. I built my college years around that belief, as if success was the only direction I was allowed to take. In doing so, I missed a lot of what college was also meant to be: exploration, fun, and becoming more than just a student. I was so focused on achievement that I forgot to simply live my teenage years. It wasn’t until my senior year that it finally shifted. I stepped out of my routine, breathed in the city air—polluted, yes, but it smelled like freedom—and I began to understand that life is also about finding yourself in different horizons, not just excelling in one. It humbled me, because I realized I had been studying life more than actually living it.

Digger Deep: Embracing Meaningful Encounters with Others

AdDU Journey Map

Looking back at my AdDU journey, I realize that beyond the academic milestones and personal growth, it has been deeply shaped by my relationship with God and the role of prayer in my everyday life. In moments of silence, whether inside the library, during recollection, or even in the quiet pauses between overwhelming tasks, I found that prayer became more than a routine; it became a grounding force that helped me regain clarity when my thoughts felt scattered and my emotions felt heavy.

This journey is beyond what it seems to be purely academe; it is also about building intrapersonal and interpersonal relationships. Along the way in reaching my dreams, I met and lost people that almost became my downfall. Yet, God shed light and turned my weaknesses into strengths that molded me into someone braver and bolder. My collage represents a journey of becoming, shaped by places, people, experiences, and values that continuously formed who I am and who I am still becoming.

Places: AdDU libraries

The AdDU Library and quiet campus spaces became my refuge. Its silence helped me regain sanity, reflect clearly, and find peace during academic and emotional overwhelm. In that stillness, I learned to slow down, listen inwardly, and reconnect with myself beyond the noise of expectations.

Events and Activities: Recollection

The recollection experience became a significant turning point in my journey. It allowed me to pause from academic pressure and reconnect with my inner self and my faith. In that moment of stillness and reflection, I was reminded of what truly matters and realigned my direction beyond achievements alone.

Ignatian Values: Magis

Through my journey, Magis became more than a concept; it became a challenge to go beyond what is required and to pursue depth, purpose, and excellence not just for achievement, but for service. I also grew in discernment, learning to reflect before acting, and in Cura Personalis, recognizing the importance of treating each person with care and respect in their uniqueness.

Moments of Becoming: Clinical Internship at RICAFI

My clinical internship at RICAFI marked my moments of becoming. Working closely with children with autism became a turning point that shaped my character and perspective. It taught me patience in silence, compassion in action, and presence in understanding. In those everyday encounters, I became more grounded, more intentional, and more aware of what it truly means to serve with empathy and purpose.

People of Gratitude: My people

I am beyond blessed to have my family, friends, and boyfriend who continuously supported me throughout my AdDU journey. Their presence gave me strength in moments of doubt and comfort in moments of exhaustion. I am also grateful for mentors, classmates, and even brief encounters with strangers who, in their own ways, shaped my Ateneo formation.
